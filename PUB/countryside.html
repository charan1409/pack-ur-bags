FEW DESTINATIONS FOR RURAL TOURISM IN INDIA

Puttur, Andhra Pradesh

This small village in Andhra Pradesh is popular for its agriculture, mangroves and the prominent silk business. The people are involved in various facets of silk saree weaving. Enjoy the grand tour of the silk saree making process, the warm hospitality of the people and the many temples that dot this little village.

Theerthamalai, Tamil Nadu

Theerthamalai in Dharmapuri district of Tamil Nadu is an emerging destination for rural tourism in the country. The name of the village translates to mean ‘The Hill of holy Water' and has a temple that dates back to the 7th century. Enjoy the calm and quiet that only such villages can provide.

₹1000.00 ₹1200.00
visit

Shyam Gaon, Assam

The small Buddhist locality in Jorhat district known as Shyam Gaon can provide an interesting rural journey in Assam. India is an interesting country, where culture and traditions change every few hundred meters. The Shyam Gaon is one such village, where the local Khamyang tribals are followers of Theravada Buddhism.

₹1000.00 ₹1200.00
visit

Chitrakote, Chhattisgarh

The tribes of India present interesting opportunities to learn about colourful cultures and a way of life far removed from the rest of the country. Chitrakote is one such village located in the Bastar district of Chhattisgarh. Popular for the folk arts and handicrafts that are made in the village, Chitrakote is also popular for the Chitrakoot waterfalls.You can also visit the Jagdalpur Palace and Danteshwari temple located here.

₹1000.00 ₹1200.00
visit

Lachen, Sikkim

This is a small village that is set against a backdrop of snow-capped mountains and conifer trees. The village that has only recently thrown open the doors to travellers, is a pristine and unexplored landscape. Get a taste of the simple life of the village folk, take a trip to the Lachen Gommpa and enjoy treks and walks in the surrounding regions.The locals are friendly and social, so you are sure to find good company in them!

₹1000.00 ₹1200.00
visit

Hodka, Gujarat

The village located in the Kutch or Kachchh district of Gujarat is a beautiful place that has a mix of both the desert of clay and sand that makes the region so unique. The region has a diverse eco-system, with the wetlands providing home to over 100 species of birds during the winter months.You can also take part in the celebrations of Sharad Utsav that is celebrated during the period of Navaratri.

₹1000.00 ₹1200.00
